This is Tic-Tac-Tic-Tac-Toe. The object is to get tic-tac-toes on the smaller boards in order to get a tic-tac-toe on the large board. The game starts with the X player playing on any of the smaller boards. The O player then plays an O on the smaller board relative to the placement of the X. For example, if the X player places an X on the top-left corner of the center board, the O player must play on the top-left board. If the O player is unable to play on the top-left board because the board is already claimed, they can play anywhere.
